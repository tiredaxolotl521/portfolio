Yo-Kai Express

Generating personas based on observations

Overview

My role

UX Reseracher

Timeline

Sept. 20 - Oct. 4, 2022

Team

Solo

Tools

HTML, CSS

Problem

Context

The objective of this project is to observe real users interacting with an interface of my choosing and create personas based on user experiences. I chose to observe people using the Yo-Kai express machine in the RISD portfolio dinning hall. From my interviews and observations, I was able to create personas and develop a storyboard.

Contextual inquiry

Contextual inquiry is a type of field study that involves in-depth observation and interviews of a small sample of users to gain a robust understanding of work practices and behaviors. For this project, I observed and interview people while they were interacting with an interface. I selected an interface in public view and observed others that would not make them uncomfortable. For this assingment, I chose to write about the Yokai hot soup machine in the RISD portfolio dining hall.

Research

Accessible interface

The accessible interface I choose was the YoKai hot noodle soup machine located in the RISD portfolio dining hall. I choose this machine because it is a complex mechanism, but has a sleek user interface and I wanted to see how RISD students interacted with this unique machine.

User experience

I selected interface in public view so that other members of the public may see what's going on. I prepared questions for users regarding their experiences with the interface that solicited insight about their expectations, experience, motives, and challenges using the interface. Then I created a sketch and briefly explain the problem that the interface is trying to solve and how its key components function.

I observed about 5 different people using the Yo-Kai Express machine. I particularly focused on how they used the interface and if there were any struggles or difficulties users encountered. After watching several people, I wrote two things in my notebook.

  1. Notes describing the key observations from observing users. This is essentially an accumulation of general patterns of interactions with the interface.
  2. List of questions and summarized responses from users that highlighted the main points or important patterns.

Personas

Empathy Maps

I then took the data gathered from the observations and interviews from users and created a persona. These personas are in the form of empathy maps of based on general trends and overarching characteristics I observe in the users I interviewed.

Story Board

To create storyboard, I used one of the personas from the empathy maps to develop a scenario where a user would normally use this interface. The storyboard aim is to present authenticity, emotion, and simplicity. The storyboard created follows my persona's journey from start to end of how they use the Yo-Kai express machine. The storyboards represents the goals of the user and illustrates the steps for using the interface.

Conclusion

Takeaways

Through this project, I had several key takeaways. First, personas are fictional characters that represent the different types of users who will be interacting with the product. By creating personas, designers can gain a deeper understanding of their users' needs, goals, and motivations, which can help guide the design process and ensure that the final product meets the needs of the target audience. Additionally, personas can help designers avoid making assumptions about their users, which can lead to design decisions that may not be effective or user-friendly. Overall, creating personas is a crucial step in the UI/UX design process, as it allows designers to create products that are tailored to the needs of their users.

Lessons learned

Creating personas is valuable for understanding user interaction in a UI/UX project. It's important to conduct thorough research and actively use personas during the design process. Personas can also encourage empathy and understanding.

One surprising idea that emerged during the project was the idea of using personas as a way to encourage empathy and understanding. By assigning characteristics and personalities to each persona, I was able to see the project from the perspective of the end user and make design decisions that would improve their experience.
